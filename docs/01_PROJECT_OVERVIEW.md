## 1. Project Introduction

EventFlow is an enterprise-level Full Stack Event Management System designed to streamline and digitalize the complete workflow of event management companies.

The platform enables customers, event managers, designers, accountants, and administrators to collaborate through a centralized web application. Instead of relying on disconnected tools such as spreadsheets, emails, messaging applications, and paper documents, EventFlow provides a unified environment where all event-related activities are managed efficiently.

The system supports the complete event lifecycle, including customer request submission, event planning, design collaboration, quotation management, task assignment, payment tracking, and post-event evaluation.

This project is developed as an enterprise-grade portfolio application that follows modern software engineering practices, including requirement analysis, software architecture design, database modeling, RESTful API development, responsive frontend development, testing, version control, and deployment.

The project aims to simulate a real-world software development process rather than serving as a simple CRUD application.

The system is designed with scalability, maintainability, and modularity in mind, allowing future integration with payment gateways, notification services, mobile applications, analytics services, and third-party APIs.

## 2. Business Background

### 2.1 Industry Overview

The event management industry has experienced remarkable growth over the past decade due to the increasing demand for professional event planning services. Businesses, organizations, and individuals now invest significantly in organizing high-quality events such as conferences, seminars, exhibitions, product launches, weddings, birthday parties, workshops, corporate meetings, and year-end celebrations.

As customer expectations continue to rise, event management companies are expected to deliver creative concepts, efficient project execution, transparent communication, and high-quality customer experiences. Consequently, the industry has gradually shifted toward digital transformation to improve operational efficiency and maintain competitiveness.

Despite these developments, many small and medium-sized event management companies still rely heavily on manual management methods. This creates significant operational limitations, especially when handling multiple events simultaneously.

---

### 2.2 Current Business Process

A typical event management company usually operates through multiple departments, including Customer Service, Event Management, Design, Accounting, and Administration.

The current business workflow generally follows these steps:

1. Customers submit event requests.
2. Event managers communicate with customers to gather detailed requirements.
3. Designers prepare event concepts, layouts, and visual materials.
4. Accountants create quotations based on customer requirements.
5. Customers review and approve the quotation.
6. The company executes the event.
7. Payments are processed according to the contract.
8. Customer feedback is collected after the event.

Although this workflow appears straightforward, the activities are often managed using multiple disconnected tools such as spreadsheets, emails, messaging applications, and paper documents instead of a centralized information system.

---

### 2.3 Existing Challenges

The current management approach introduces several operational challenges that affect both internal collaboration and customer satisfaction.

Common challenges include:

- Customer information is stored in different files.
- Event schedules are manually maintained.
- Employee assignments are difficult to coordinate.
- Design files are exchanged through messaging applications.
- Quotation revisions are difficult to track.
- Payment records are scattered across multiple documents.
- Managers have limited visibility into project progress.
- Communication between departments is inefficient.
- Important information may be duplicated or lost.
- Reporting and performance analysis require significant manual effort.

As the number of concurrent events increases, these challenges become more severe and negatively impact operational efficiency.

---

### 2.4 Business Pain Points

Based on the existing workflow, several critical business pain points can be identified.

The major pain points include:

- Lack of centralized information management.
- Manual and repetitive administrative tasks.
- Poor collaboration among departments.
- Difficulty monitoring project progress.
- Limited transparency for customers.
- Slow quotation approval process.
- Increased operational costs.
- Higher risk of human errors.
- Inconsistent customer experience.
- Difficulty generating business reports and statistics.

These issues reduce productivity, delay project execution, and limit the company's ability to deliver high-quality services consistently.

---

### 2.5 Need for Digital Transformation

Digital transformation has become an essential strategy for service-based businesses seeking to improve efficiency and remain competitive.

For event management companies, implementing an integrated management platform provides numerous advantages, including:

- Standardized business processes.
- Centralized information management.
- Real-time collaboration between departments.
- Improved communication with customers.
- Faster quotation preparation and approval.
- Better task monitoring and project tracking.
- Reduced administrative workload.
- Improved decision-making through real-time data.
- Enhanced customer experience.

Furthermore, a centralized platform creates opportunities for future integration with payment gateways, notification services, mobile applications, analytics platforms, and third-party business systems.

---

### 2.6 Proposed Solution

To overcome the limitations of traditional event management, EventFlow is proposed as an integrated Event Management System that digitalizes the entire event organization lifecycle.

The platform centralizes customer requests, event planning, employee assignment, task management, design collaboration, quotation generation, payment tracking, and project monitoring into a single web-based application.

Through role-based access control, standardized workflows, centralized data management, and real-time project visibility, EventFlow enables customers and internal departments to collaborate more efficiently while reducing operational complexity.

The proposed solution is designed with scalability, maintainability, and extensibility in mind, allowing future expansion through additional modules, third-party integrations, and emerging technologies as business requirements evolve.

## 10. Non-functional Requirements

The EventFlow system shall satisfy the following non-functional requirements to ensure reliability, security, maintainability, and scalability.

### 10.1 Performance

- The system should respond to user requests within 2 seconds under normal operating conditions.
- Dashboard statistics should be generated efficiently without noticeable delays.
- The platform should support multiple concurrent users without significant performance degradation.

---

### 10.2 Security

- All users must be authenticated before accessing protected resources.
- JWT shall be used for authentication.
- Passwords must be encrypted before storage.
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) shall restrict access according to user roles.
- Sensitive data shall be transmitted using HTTPS in production.

---

### 10.3 Availability

- The system should be available 24/7 except during scheduled maintenance.
- Data backup should be performed regularly.
- Unexpected failures should not result in permanent data loss.

---

### 10.4 Scalability

- The architecture should support future expansion.
- New modules can be added without affecting existing functionality.
- The system should support future deployment in cloud environments.

---

### 10.5 Maintainability

- The project should follow clean architecture principles.
- Source code should be modular and easy to maintain.
- API endpoints should follow RESTful standards.
- Documentation should be maintained throughout the project lifecycle.

---

### 10.6 Usability

- The user interface should be intuitive and responsive.
- Navigation should be simple and consistent.
- Users should be able to complete common tasks with minimal training.

---

### 10.7 Reliability

- System data should remain consistent after each transaction.
- Errors should be properly handled and logged.
- Unexpected failures should not corrupt business data.
